QUESTIONS YOU MIGHT HAVE

What equipment do I need?

You'll need Ableton Live, a reliable computer, headphones or monitor speakers, and a journal. A MIDI controller is helpful but not essential. The focus of our work together is more on your creative approach than specific gear or plugins.

I'm worried my music isn't "good enough" yet. Should I wait until I improve more?

I hear this concern often, and it's exactly the mindset shift we address in the course. The truth is, your music becomes "good enough" through the process of finishing and releasing it, not before. If you have a solid understanding of your DAW basics and have been producing for at least 6 months, you're ready. This course is about transforming your relationship with music, not achieving some arbitrary technical standard first.

How is this different from all the tutorials and courses available online?

Most production education focuses solely on technical knowledge—another plugin, another technique, another shortcut. What's missing is the integration of those skills with your unique creative voice and a sustainable creative practice. Heart of Sound bridges this gap by addressing both the technical and the intuitive, the practical and the philosophical. Plus, you're getting direct access to me and a supportive community, not just passive video content.

What if I miss a lesson?

Life happens, and I've designed the course with that in mind. All sessions are recorded and permanently available for you to rewatch. My teaching assistant and I also provide session summaries with key points, so you can quickly catch up even if you miss a live session.

How much time will I need each week?

You should plan for 5-10 hours weekly, including 4 hours of live calls plus at least 1-6 hours for assignments. I've found this is the sweet spot that allows for meaningful progress without overwhelming your schedule. The course is designed to integrate with your life, not consume it.

I worry I won't be able to keep up with others in the course. What if I'm behind?

This isn't a competition—it's a personal journey supported by a community. Each producer moves at their own pace, and I welcome and celebrate that diversity. Some of my most successful students initially felt they were "behind," only to discover their unique perspective was exactly what made their music special. I'm there to meet you where you are.
