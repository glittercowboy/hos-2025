A recent study by Harvard estimated that 99.99% of house music producers never finish any of the tracks they start. They go on to state that every month 47.3 billion project files get relegated to a folder in a folder in a folder called "Works In Progress" never to see the light of day again.

One could get philosophical about it and say that it's actually rather beautiful that all of these intimate moments of musical connection between producers and their creations stay private; forever remaining "could have beens" rather than music that is shared and experienced by the... god forbid... mere mortal 'listeners' of the world.

However, I have a sneaking suspicion that the reason most producers do not finish and share the music they seemingly spend so much time on is NOT because of some meta statement about the divide between le artiste and the consumer. I would even hazard a guess that most producers would actually quite enjoy the experience of finishing and sharing their music so that their efforts can be enjoyed by others.

So why is it then that so few songs get finished and so many producers remain bedroom tinkerers rather than artists that actually grow fanbases around their passion?

Because most producers are big fat fucking PERFECTIONISTS incapable of shushing their monkey minds and thus creating art from whence it should really come...
